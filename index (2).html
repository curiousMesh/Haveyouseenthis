This Week's Question
"Be fruitful and multiply" — but were Adam and Eve even capable of having children in the Garden?

Most read "be fruitful and multiply" and assume it's a straightforward command to have children. But something sits unresolved beneath the surface. Adam and Eve were in a state of innocence. Their biology, their understanding of intimacy, their very consciousness — all of it was untested. Was this a command they could even execute from where they stood?

And then there's this — they were placed in a garden. Not a city. Not a nation. A garden. The mathematics of multiplying from two people in one garden into a civilization is worth sitting with. The command already assumed what hadn't happened yet.
